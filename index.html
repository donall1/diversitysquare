Troy Bastian

Troy Bastian is a journalist, author, and screenwriter
whose work spans 35 years. He has written for numerous
newspapers and magazines including Sports Illustrated
and Archaeology.

His 2015 novel, Slot Machine Miracles, is based on the experiences
of the "mole people" who live in the underground flood tunnels of
Las Vegas. In 2019, Troy Published Bottles and Diapers: Young Alcoholics
on Love, a compilation of his poetry and short stories.

Troy hails from Charleston, South Carolina, where he got
his start in the newspaper business at the weekly Edisto Islander.
He went on to work for newspapers in South Carolina, Florida,
Nevada, California, and Washington. He has received awards and
recognition from the American Newspaper Publisher's, the Washington
Press Association, and from the Nevada Press Association.

In addition to books and newspapers, Troy has written several
screenplays, produced two documentary films, and a television
movie, Armed and Innocent. His documentary, The Survivors: Growing
Up with Spina Bifida, is about teenagers living with physical challenges.
His second documentary, Beyond the Shadows, is a companion piece for
Slot Machine Miracles, where Bastian explores the drain system and
comes face to face with the real people who live there.

Troy resides in Las Vegas where he is working on development of a
feature-length film based on Slot Machine Miracles.
